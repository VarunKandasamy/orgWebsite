**Research Department**  
   
Introduction:  
The Research Department supports \_\_\_\_\_ mission to strengthen participation in local politics across the United States. Led by Director Lauren Elle Darby, the department conducts research that helps those involved with the organization as well as our internal departments so that they understand local politics as well as expand into new communities and work to engage young people in local politics.  
 

What we do:  
We currently operate in four different states, New Jersey, Oklahoma, Massachusetts, and Georgia. The research department serves as an innovative hub to analyze the histories, and diverse political landscapes of the localities in which the organization operates out of. Our research informs internal strategy while generating suggestions on how we can evolve. Additionally, our research is shared with donors, sponsors, and government officials to create a   
tangible change in local policies as we work to provide evidence-based recommendations.   
   
**Our Vision**  
   
Over this next year, this department will undergo a transition to operate as a think-tank that will work to go beyond providing internal research, but we will produce extensive research that will influence the future of local politics. 

As this transition progresses, this department will provide both city- and state-level briefings and reports. These materials will inform discussions on how local government can best serve the people they represent. The team includes the Director and seven Associate Directors: History, Civic Research and Local Politics, Curriculum and Chapter Education, Progress, Finance, Data Collection and Analysis, and Ethnographic Research. The Associate Directors each lead their specialized research areas. The Director synthesizes their findings into cohesive reports and recommendations for officials. Through this design, the Research Department for The America Project of Civic Engagement works to produce insightful, research-driven information on local politics and civic engagement nationwide.

**Our Team**

To operate, the Research Department is made up of a Director and seven different subdivisions:

1. History: The history subdivision works to find out the history, culture, and demographics of each locality that we currently operate out of as well as the ones that we are looking to expand into. Through the work of the Associate Director and a team that researches all areas of the United States, they regularly perform in-depth analyses of the political, social, and cultural aspects of our locality.   
2.  Civic Research and Local Politics: The civic research and local politics subdivision finds out what is going on in all our localities’ local governments and evaluates what matters to the people. They investigate issues that are of concern to both the majority and the minority of people in these communities. As a part of this they take note of the diverse landscape of every region we are involved in and see how these demographics tell a story of the way the local governments are today.  
3. Curriculum and Chapter Education: The curriculum and chapter education subdivision works to develop a set curriculum for all of our college chapters to follow. They work in tandem with our Chapter’s program, led by Director Ben Kelley, as they evaluate the best way to teach students about local politics. This curriculum is then adapted to best serve students of different colleges from different regions in the United States.  
4. Progress: The progress subdivision discovers innovative ways for the organization to move forward. By using evidence curated by the rest of the research department, they are responsible for providing recommendations on how The America Project can best move forward.  
5. Finance: The finance subdivision evaluates the department’s expenses and how these are used in tandem with the rest of the organization. This subdivision is responsible for managing the department’s budget and how it is spent over time.  
6. Ethnographic Research: The ethnographic research subdivision is responsible for performing a qualitative analysis of the people within our localities to gauge what they view as the issues in our society. By performing on the ground research, they go out and talk to people to ensure that we as an organization are fulfilling their needs and not spreading misinformation about what other’s may view their needs as.
