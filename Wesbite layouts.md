 1\. Mission and vision: 

Mission: Demopolis aims to foster and encourage Americans’ active participation in local governance and government. We aim to reinvigorate local political and civic life, help people build and thrive in their local communities, and combat narratives of harmful local division to ensure the long-term welfare of Americans and their communities. 

**Our Long-term Vision:** We hope to build a community of partners and members across the non-profit, private, and governmental sectors that support our goals and each other. We are building a movement for sustained, collaborative, and comprehensive civic participation.

 2\. What we do and how we do it: 

## **What We Do and Believe**

We are a non-partisan organization founded with the hope of engaging all Americans in local politics and civic affairs.

### **Our Priorities**

* **Youth Engagement:** A priority focus of our organization is engaging young people in local politics and civic organizations through direct participation (e.g. internships with a city councilmember), especially in overlooked parts of America.   
* **Community Creation:** A focus for our organization involves creating spaces in communities with community members where citizens can gather to discuss civic issues and engage in local political and civic actions in their neighborhood (e.g. book clubs, meeting groups for issues etc).  
* **Supporting Local Political Institutions:** We aim to assist local governments and civic organizations in ways that strengthen underfunded and understaffed local political institutions (e.g. sending college interns, rallying community members to volunteer at local government offices). In doing so, we bring governments closer to the people and make the people a more integral part of their government.  
* **Community Safety Nets:** We want to help communities build robust safety nets for themselves. Consequently, we encourage all Americans to directly participate in local political institutions (e.g. attending town hall meetings, voting in local elections) and grow community organizations and charitable institutions.  
* **Community Stakeholders:** We subscribe to the idea that community institutions are a vital part of any support network. We understand that when people have a meaningful stake in their community, they live more productive lives, leading to less destruction of themselves and their environments. We will with our partners to make everyone have a meaningful stake in their local civic and political community.   
* **Generational Impact:** A core tenet of our organization is to advocate for future generations. Whether building generational wealth or robust political and civic community, they all take time to bloom. We are committed to helping every American community begin the work of making an active political and civic community today, even if the full effects are not realized for generations.

What we offer:

For colleges and universities: we offer a chapters program that any interested student can set up and join. 

For local non-profits and governmental institutions: We offer our resources on locality specific insights and actionable recommendations. We also offer a group of enthusiastic members that can intern and partner with your institutions in many ways to work towards both of our missions, and we can also offer tailored services through our programs to help your organizations to better serve your local community.

For the public at large: We offer specific information and resources on your local cities and towns to help you know your civic and political institutions and processes better. 

### **Our Approach**

We emphasize the importance of listening to community members and stakeholders everywhere we go across America. By listening to stakeholders, we best understand how to partner with them to enable more direct and fruitful participation in civic and political life.

**Some questions that drive our work include:**

* Why do people not get involved in civic affairs or local politics?  
* How can we encourage people to participate more fully in their community rather than, for example, staying home and on Instagram?  
* How can we encourage people to read to their children and teach them about politics?
